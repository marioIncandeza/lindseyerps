    Project 0

    Starter project for EENG 3530

    Parts

    • Photocell
    • NPN transistor
    • LED
    • 220Ω resistor
    • 10kΩ potentiometer
    • 9V battery

    Schematic

    Overhead View

    This is a simple circuit I threw together which controls an LED. The potentiometer can be adjusted such that the transistor will switch the LED on/off. While the LED is on, an intense application of light to the face of the photoresistor causes the LED to dim. The resistance of the photoresistor decreases with increasing light intensity. When the LED is on, it is effectively in parallel with the potentiometer and photocell. As the resistance of the photocell decreases, the amount of current flowing through it increases, and consequently, the current must decrease through the LED causing the light to dim. Although I don't know much about the NPN transistor, my guess is that it serves primarily as a switch here, opening and closing based on the voltage applied at the base (B) terminal.
